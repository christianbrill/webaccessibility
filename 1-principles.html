Here are some of the fundamental principles of web accessibility when coding a website

Provide Alternatives

Put yourself in someone else’s shoes and image what sort of alternatives would be good to have if you had some certain kind of disability. For example, don’t ever include <img> without the "alt" attribute because this very attribute is what will be read by the likes of ChromeVox etc.

Be Welcoming

Don’t discriminate against anyone. Try to include everyone and make your websites as warm and welcoming as possible.

Be To-the-point

Don’t dilly-dally your way through the website and confuse your users. Be concise where you can be and present information in a clear and obvious manner.

Be Informative

Always tell the user where they are on the page. Inform them about their navigation and provide them with a way of being able to find what they’re looking for on your website.

Be Consistent

Stick to certain rules and guidelines. This applies to the way you create your website as much as it does to the way you make it accessible.
